Import RADARSAT Constellation Mission (RCM) Product

The RADARSAT Constellation Mission (RCM) is a C-band SAR constellation of 3 satellites operated by the Canadian Space Agency (CSA). The constellation provides a 4-day revisit at mid-latitudes and daily coverage at high Arctic latitudes.

Operational: 2019-06-12 to present (3 satellites: RCM-1, RCM-2, RCM-3)

Frequency Band: C-band (5.405 GHz)

Acquisition Modes:

  • Spotlight (~1 m x 3 m resolution, ~20 km swath)
  • Very High Resolution 3m (~3 m, ~20 km swath)
  • High Resolution 5m (~5 m, ~30 km swath)
  • Medium Resolution 16m (~16 m, ~30-40 km swath)
  • Medium Resolution 30m ScanSAR (~30 m, ~125 km swath)
  • Medium Resolution 50m ScanSAR (~50 m, ~350 km swath)
  • Low Resolution 100m ScanSAR (~100 m, ~500 km swath)
  • Low Noise (~100 m, ~350 km, optimized NESZ)
  • Ship Detection (~40 m, ~350 km)
  • Quad-Pol (~9 m, ~20 km)

Product Types:

  • SLC Single Look Complex (slant range, complex I/Q data)
  • GRC Ground Range Complex (ground range, complex data)
  • GCC Ground Range Geocoded Complex
  • GRD Ground Range Detected (multi-looked amplitude)
  • GCD Ground Range Geocoded Detected
  • MLC Multi-Look Covariance (covariance matrix: C11, C12, C22)

Polarizations:

  • Single Pol (HH or VV)
  • Dual Pol (HH+HV or VV+VH)
  • Compact Polarimetry (RH+RV — circular transmit, linear receive)
  • Full Quad-Pol (HH+HV+VH+VV, dedicated mode only)

Compact Polarimetry (CP): RCM's signature capability. Transmits right-hand circular polarization and receives on H and V linear channels simultaneously. Available across all wide-swath modes. Enables Stokes parameter decomposition and approximate full-polarimetric analysis.

Calibration: Products include per-pixel LUT files (lutSigma.xml, lutBeta.xml, lutGamma.xml) for radiometric calibration. Noise floor annotations are provided in noiseLevels.xml.

Opening products: Products can be opened by selecting the manifest.safe file, the product directory, or a ZIP archive. The reader supports all product types and polarization modes.

Data Access:
