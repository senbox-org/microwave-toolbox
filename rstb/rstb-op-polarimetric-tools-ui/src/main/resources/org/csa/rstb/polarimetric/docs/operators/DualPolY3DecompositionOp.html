Dual-pol Y3-DP / iDPSV Decomposition

Three-Component Decomposition for Dual-pol C2 Data

Decomposes a dual-pol covariance matrix C2 into surface (Ps), double-bounce (Pd) and volume (Pv) scattering powers via a model-based approach analogous to Freeman–Durden but adapted to two channels.

Volume model

The volume contribution is parameterised by a normalised diagonal matrix V = diag(a, b). The amplitude fv is fixed by matching the cross-pol intensity c22:

    fv = c22 / b
    Pv = fv · (a + b)

Three volume models are offered:

Model(a, b)Typical use
Uniform random(½, ½)Symmetric scatterers (S1 IW VV+VH)
Bragg-like(⅔, ⅓)Vegetation-over-surface
Dipole cloud(⅜, ⅛)Oriented-dipole canopy (HH+HV)

Surface vs double-bounce branch

After volume removal, the residual matrix Cr = C2 − fv·V has its cross-pol diagonal forced to zero (rank-1 residual). The remaining trace is assigned to one of two scattering mechanisms based on the sign of the real part of the off-diagonal element:

  • Re(c12) ≥ 0 ⇒ surface dominant: Ps = trace(Cr), Pd = 0.
  • Re(c12) < 0 ⇒ double-bounce dominant: Pd = trace(Cr), Ps = 0.

Volume over-estimation (negative residual diagonal) is detected and clipped: fv is reduced so that c11 − fv·a = 0, preserving non-negative powers.

Inputs

  • Dual-pol C2 covariance matrix product (4 bands: c11, c12_real, c12_imag, c22).

Outputs

  • Ps_Y3DP — surface scattering power.
  • Pd_Y3DP — double-bounce power.
  • Pv_Y3DP — volume power.
