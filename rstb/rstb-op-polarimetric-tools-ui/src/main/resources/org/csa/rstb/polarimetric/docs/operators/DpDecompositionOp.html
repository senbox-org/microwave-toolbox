1. Dual-pol Scattering Powers Formulation (Decomposition method)

The dual-pol scattering powers (decomposition method) are calculated from the processed dual-pol 2x2 wave covariance C2 matrix.

This method is based on the two dual-pol indices: DpRBI and DpRSI. First, a dual-pol target characteristic parameter is computed using DpRBI and DpRSI. It varies between 0° and 90°, with low values close to 0° indicating "surface-like" targets, high values close to 90° describing "dihedral-like", and intermediate values close to 45° representing mixed targets [1].

Subsequently, the degree of polarization and target characteristic parameter are together employed to split the total power into "Dihedral-like" (Pd-l), "Surface-like" (Ps-l), unpolarized (Pu) component as shown in the figure below and in [1].

It is important to note that hills or mountains facing the radar produce strong backscatter and are often confused with built-up areas, resulting in high DpRBI values. Therefore, we employ a slope layer to mask such areas to avoid misinterpretations over such areas. Please include a slope layer along with the C2 matrix (Raster -> DEM Tools -> Compute Slope and Aspect).
