RVoG 3-Stage Inversion Operator

Cloude & Papathanassiou 2003 three-stage inversion of PolInSAR coherence for forest height. Improves on the SinC-only RVoG Forest Height by explicitly extracting the ground-only coherence point and using it to reduce ground bias in the volume coherence estimate.

Algorithm

  1. Stage 1. Read three complex polarimetric coherences γk = ik + j*qk (typically the three outputs of CoherenceOptimization with outputInComplex=true).
  2. Stage 2 (line fit). Fit a straight line through the three coherence points in the complex plane (total least squares). The line crosses the unit circle at two points; the one closer to the lowest-magnitude observation is adopted as the ground coherence γg.
  3. Stage 3 (inversion). Take the observation furthest from γg as the volume-pure coherence γv; SinC-invert |γv| for canopy height hv.

Inputs

  • Three pairs of (ik, qk) complex coherence bands.
  • Optional kz band or scene-constant kz.

Outputs

  • forestHeight [m].
  • ground_coh_mag = |γg|.
  • volume_coh_mag = |γv|.

References

  • Cloude S. R., Papathanassiou K. P. (2003). DOI 10.1049/ip-rsn:20030449.
  • Cloude S. R. (2010). Polarimetric SAR Interferometry. IEEE TGRS 48(8), 2957-2972 (3SI consolidation).
