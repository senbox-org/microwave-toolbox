Import NISAR Product

Mission: NASA-ISRO Synthetic Aperture Radar (NISAR) — joint NASA/ISRO Earth-observation mission.

Frequency Bands: L-band (LSAR, 1.257 GHz) plus S-band (SSAR, 3.20 GHz) — dual-frequency simultaneous acquisition.

Polarizations: Full quad-pol (HH, HV, VH, VV) plus dual-pol and compact-pol modes.

Repeat Cycle: 12 days, with global coverage every 12 days for solid-earth, ecosystems and cryosphere applications.

File Format: HDF5 (NetCDF-4 readable). Plugin auto-detects products with filenames beginning NISAR_* or WINNIP_* (the pre-launch JPL simulation family using the same schema).

Frequency Bands (A and B)

NISAR can acquire on two frequency sub-bands simultaneously. When a product carries both, the reader exposes:

  • Frequency A bands with no suffix — e.g. i_HH, Intensity_HH.
  • Frequency B bands with _S suffix — e.g. i_HH_S, Intensity_HH_S.

Compound Complex Decoding

RSLC and GSLC datasets are stored as HDF5 compound types containing real / imaginary pairs. NISAR uses both float32 (8 byte) and float16 (4 byte) compound layouts; the reader detects the element size and falls back to manual Float.float16ToFloat(short) conversion when ucar.nc2 can't decode the half-precision compound natively.

Geocoding

L2 (geocoded) products carry coordinate arrays in either geographic (lat/lon, EPSG:4326) or UTM-projected coordinates. The reader detects which case applies via the units attribute on the coordinate variable and converts UTM to lat/lon as needed for the SNAP tie-point geocoding.

Radiometric Calibration

RSLC products are radiometrically uncalibrated. Apply the SNAP Radiometric Calibration operator with the BIOMASS / NISAR profile to convert to sigma-naught, beta-naught or gamma-naught using the per-polarisation calibration constants embedded in the HDF5 calibrationInformation group.

GSLC, GCOV, GUNW, GOFF, RIFG, RUNW, ROFF and SME2 are rejected by the calibrator — GCOV is already in calibrated covariance units, GSLC is complex-calibrated by the upstream geocoder, the rest are derived interferometric / offset products that don't carry the calibration constants, and SME2 is a geophysical product (soil moisture).

Doppler Centroid

NISAR provides a 2-D Doppler centroid LUT (azimuth-time × slant-range) in the processing-parameters group. The reader emits one dop_coef_list.N entry per azimuth-time sample so downstream operators that interpolate Doppler over the acquisition (e.g. InSAR azimuth deramping) have the full time-variation, not just a single representative row.
