Gaofen-3 is a Chinese C-band SAR satellite constellation operated by CRESDA (China Centre for Resources Satellite Data and Application). The constellation consists of three satellites providing approximately 3-4 day revisit.

Satellites:

  • GF-3 (launched 2016-08-10, operational)
  • GF-3B (launched 2022-04-07, operational)
  • GF-3C (launched 2024-12, operational)

Frequency Band: C-band (5.4 GHz)

Acquisition Modes (12 modes):

  • Spotlight [SL] (~1 m resolution)
  • Ultra-fine stripmap [UFS] (~3 m)
  • Fine stripmap [FSI] (~5 m)
  • Wide fine stripmap [FSII] (~10 m)
  • Standard stripmap [SS] (~25 m)
  • Narrow ScanSAR [NSC] (~50 m)
  • Wide ScanSAR [WSC] (~100 m)
  • Global observation [GLO] (~500 m)
  • Quad-pol stripmap [QPSI] (~8 m)
  • Wide quad-pol stripmap [QPSII] (~25 m)
  • Wave [WAV]
  • Extended incidence angle [EXT]

Product Types:

  • L1A Single-look complex (SLC)
  • L1B Single-look / Multi-look image products (detected)
  • L2 Geocoded products

Polarizations: Single (HH or VV), Dual (HH+HV, VV+VH), Quad (HH+HV+VH+VV)

File Format: GeoTIFF imagery with XML metadata (.meta.xml) and RPC geocoding parameters (.rpc file).

Opening products: Products can be opened by selecting the .meta.xml metadata file or the product directory. Opening zipped products (*.zip) directly is also supported. All three satellites (GF-3, GF-3B, GF-3C) use the same product format.

Data Access:

  • CRESDA: https://osdds.cresda.com
  • PIE-Engine: https://pie.cresda.cn
